Calculus

  • Calculus is the mathematical study of continuous change.
  • Infinitesimal calculus was developed independently in the late 17th century by Isaac Newton and Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz.

           Today,   calculus   has widespread uses in science, engineering, and social science. For example, it can be used with linear algebra to find the "best fit" linear approximation for a set of points in a domain. Maxwell's theory of electromagnetism and Einstein's theory of general relativity are also expressed in the language of differential calculus. In economics, calculus allows for the determination of maximal profit by providing a way to easily calculate both marginal cost and marginal revenue.

Matrices

  • Matrices are rectangular arrays of integers with rows and columns.
  • Arthur Cayley was(16/08/1821) a great mathematician and known as the father of matrices. In 1858, Arthur Cayley published the theoretical definition of the matrix in his Memoir on the Theory of Matrices.
  • Cayley first applied them to the study of systems of linear equations.

          Matrices   have many practical uses in everyday life. For example, most computers have numeric values displayed as two-dimensional lists on their screens,it is a matrix. And as well, matrix find its application in spreadsheets, Simulations, Physics, Genetics, Cognitive science, Convex functions. Matrices are also used in robotics and automation to help with the computations of different objects in the world.
